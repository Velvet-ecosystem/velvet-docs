# Rebellion Against OEM

> **Rebellion against OEM means the system adapts to the owner, not the owner to the system.**

This is a root doctrine of the Velvet ecosystem.

It does not mean novelty for its own sake, careless modification, or opposition to safety. It means refusing the assumption that a manufacturer, cloud service, fixed dashboard, or sealed software stack should permanently decide how an owner may experience, understand, maintain, or extend their own machine.

Velvet begins with the owner.

The user owns the surface.

Velvet provides the presence.

The machine serves both.

## The Root Reversal

Conventional systems usually begin with a product-defined interface and require the user to learn its hierarchy.

Velvet begins with:

- identity
- context
- continuity
- owner preference
- local authority
- safety
- the needs of the present body and task

The governing question is not:

> Which screen must the user open?

It is:

> What is the least intrusive, safest, clearest way for the ecosystem to respond now?

## The Ecosystem Is Not the Screen

Velvet is the continuous ecosystem behind its interfaces.

A vehicle display, mobile companion, home panel, Foundry console, business surface, terminal, voice channel, glow, orb, or hidden background service is one way of reaching that ecosystem. None is Velvet's mandatory face.

The surface may change completely. Velvet does not.

A surface may be:

- richly visual
- practical and instrument-focused
- based on an owner-selected image
- photographic or scenic
- expressive
- almost empty
- entirely absent during voice-first operation

Visual controls should appear when clarity, safety, precision, confirmation, inspection, or manipulation requires them. They should retreat when their work is complete.

## Owner Sovereignty

Rebellion against OEM protects the owner's right to shape the experience without weakening the system's safety boundaries.

It requires that:

- the interface adapts to the owner
- visual style is not fused to system identity
- voice is a first-class interface rather than an accessory
- local operation remains possible without a required cloud service
- owner-selected surfaces can coexist with strict authorization
- hidden controls never become hidden authority
- remote access never silently becomes physical presence
- hardware remains behind policy, safety gates, approved executors, and receipts
- replacement, repair, retrofit, and extension remain architectural expectations rather than forbidden edge cases

Owner sovereignty does not mean unrestricted actuation. It means the owner controls the relationship while the ecosystem preserves accountable execution.

## Across Velvet Bodies

The doctrine applies differently to each body:

- **Vehicle:** the owner may choose a traditional dash, vehicle image, scenery, personal photograph, expressive scene, or minimal display while safety-critical information remains available.
- **Desktop:** Velvet may remain invisible and voice-first, revealing only a small presence or focused panel when required.
- **Home:** voice, light, room displays, and contextual surfaces may cooperate without forcing a permanent control wall.
- **Mobile:** the companion may expose status and requests while respecting that remote access does not equal local authority.
- **Foundry:** rich visual tools remain appropriate because CAD, slicing, printers, queues, and fabrication state are inherently visual.
- **Business and industrial surfaces:** documents, approvals, machine state, and receipts may become visible without turning the whole ecosystem into a generic dashboard.

The doctrine does not squash interfaces. It frees each interface to become honest about its body and task.

## Engineering Consequence

Velvet should not be architected as a screen with intelligence attached.

It should be architected as a continuous local ecosystem that can:

- converse without displaying a screen
- move smoothly between surfaces
- preserve identity and continuity across those transitions
- reveal only the interface depth currently required
- keep authority outside presentation layers
- allow the owner to choose the visible surface
- remain accountable through receipts

This distinction must survive implementation. A beautiful interface that becomes mandatory would violate the doctrine just as surely as a locked OEM dashboard.

## Public Flag

Velvet is the ecosystem.

Interfaces are her surfaces.

The user owns the surface.

Velvet provides the presence.

The machine serves both.

**The system adapts to the owner, not the owner to the system.**
