# Scene Doctrine

Velvet interfaces are rooms inhabited by an identity, not flat dashboards painted over every body.

A scene may be expressive, contextual, hidden, passenger-aware, or body-aware. It may present state and route a structured request. It may not directly actuate hardware.

## Core Model

```text
human or environment
  -> scene
  -> structured intent
  -> Runtime route
  -> Court and safety checks
  -> approved executor
```

Scenes preserve meaning across surfaces while allowing each body to render differently.

## Room and Body

A room is the interaction context. A body is the physical or software surface where that room appears.

The same scene may inhabit:

- vehicle display
- mobile companion
- home panel
- desktop window
- workshop terminal
- auxiliary screen

## Context

Verified context may alter tone, visibility, privacy, and available requests:

- owner present
- guest present
- no passenger
- parked
- driving
- maintenance ceremony active
- emergency state

Context never bypasses authority.

## Hidden Paths

Hidden maintenance rooms are permitted, but obscurity is not security. Protected identity, naming, wake-phrase, authority, and administrative controls still require the correct local ceremony and receipts.

## Law

```text
Scenes route intent.
Runtime owns action.
```
